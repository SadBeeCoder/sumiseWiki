"Call of Cthulhu" is a tabletop role-playing game (RPG) based on the works of the famous horror author H.P. Lovecraft. In this dark and atmospheric game, players take on the roles of investigators who confront mysterious and often supernatural secrets and conspiracies. The game world is characterized by cosmic horror, where the boundary between reality and madness blurs. Players solve puzzles, investigate unexplained incidents, and encounter terrifying creatures from another dimension. The focus is on a grim atmosphere, a deep narrative, and the challenge of maintaining one's own mental health while confronting the abyss of the unknown. "Call of Cthulhu" provides an intense and unique role-playing experience that immerses players in a world of horror and mysteries.

Rulesystem

The rules system of "Call of Cthulhu" is designed to immerse players in a world of mystery and horror, reflecting the themes of Lovecraftian cosmic dread. The game uses a percentile-based system, where characters' abilities and skills are represented by percentile values. When attempting actions, players roll percentile dice to determine success or failure, with higher values indicating better performance. Characters' skills and attributes are defined by a wide range of characteristics, such as Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence, and specialized skills like Investigation, Occult, and Firearms. As the game emphasizes investigation and storytelling, combat is often deadly and discouraged, reinforcing the idea that knowledge and cautious exploration are key to survival. One unique feature of the game is the Sanity mechanic. Characters are exposed to horrific and otherworldly experiences, and as they witness the unimaginable, their sanity can deteriorate. The game master (Keeper) carefully tracks and assigns sanity points, and when a character's sanity erodes, it can result in temporary or permanent mental conditions, affecting gameplay and role-playing. "Call of Cthulhu" encourages immersive storytelling and focuses on narrative-driven gameplay. It allows players to delve into the psychological and emotional aspects of their characters, bringing out the fear and vulnerability that comes with confronting the unknown. This rules system creates an atmosphere of tension and suspense, making each decision and action a critical one as investigators strive to uncover the secrets lurking in the shadows.

"How to set up the game "

Choose a Scenario: Pick an adventure that suits players' preferences and skill levels, from investigating haunted houses to uncovering cosmic horrors.
Create Characters: Players craft investigator characters, distributing skills and attributes with a 1920s backdrop.
Gather Materials: Get rulebooks, character sheets, dice, maps, and any scenario-specific items.
Set the Mood: Create ambiance with lighting, music, and props for mystery and suspense.
Introduce Setting: Describe the 1920s era and location to set the stage.
Provide Hooks: Present a compelling reason for investigators to engage, like a mysterious letter.
Guide the Story: As Keeper, present clues, NPCs, and challenges to build tension.
Manage Sanity: Track sanity points as investigators encounter the unknown.
Encourage Role-Playing: Focus on character interactions and emotions.
Resolve Conflicts: Use the percentile-based system for skill checks, combat, and sanity tests.
Reveal the Truth: Gradually unveil mysteries while maintaining an air of uncertainty.
Conclude and Reflect: Wrap up the scenario and discuss the experience.
The goal is an immersive, collaborative storytelling experience that captures Lovecraftian horror.

Requirements for players

Imagination: Players should bring their creativity and imagination to immerse themselves in a world of mystery and horror.
Role-Playing: A willingness to embrace their investigator characters, making decisions and reactions true to the 1920s setting and Lovecraftian themes.
Teamwork: Collaborative problem-solving and sharing of clues to collectively uncover the story's secrets.
Suspenseful Mindset: Players who enjoy tension, suspense, and psychological challenges will thrive in the game's atmospheric setting.
Openness to Horror: An appreciation for cosmic horror and the unknown, as "Call of Cthulhu" delves into unsettling and eerie narratives.

5 things you need to start

Rulebooks: Acquire the core rulebook of "Call of Cthulhu" to understand the game mechanics and rules
Dice: Obtain percentile dice (d10s) for resolving actions, tests, and determining outcomes.
Character Sheets: Print or obtain character sheets to record investigator details, skills, and attributes.
Scenario Materials: Secure scenario-specific materials like maps, handouts, or props for an immersive experience.
Imagination and Enthusiasm: Bring a sense of imagination and enthusiasm to fully engage in the world of cosmic horror and suspenseful storytelling.
